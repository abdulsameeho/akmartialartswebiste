Karate

Karate is a Japanese martial art that focuses on developing defensive and counterattacking body movements. It's an unarmed discipline that uses kicking, striking, and defensive blocking with arms and legs. The emphasis is on concentrating as much of the body's power as possible at the point of impact. The core basics of karate are taught during the first three to four months. Regular training in karate can help a student learn the basics in one year. Good form and timing with basic techniques are demonstrated by the student within six months to one-and-a-half years of training. Self defence

Self-defense training is a great way to protect yourself and your loved ones. It can teach you how to defend yourself against a variety of attacks, and it can also give you the confidence to stand up for yourself.

Here are some of the benefits of self-defense training from our institute:

Increased self-confidence: Self-defense training can help you to develop your self- confidence and self-esteem. When you learn how to defend yourself, you will feel more confident and capable of taking care of yourself. Improved physical fitness: Self-defense training can help you to improve your physical fitness. Martial arts and other types of self-defense training can help you to lose weight, tone your muscles, and improve your cardiovascular health. Reduced stress levels: Self-defense training can help you to reduce stress and anxiety. When you learn how to defend yourself, you will feel more in control of your life and you will be less likely to feel stressed or anxious. Increased self-awareness: Self-defense training can help you to become more aware of your surroundings and more likely to recognize potential threats. This awareness can help you to stay safe and avoid dangerous situations. Learned skills that can be used in everyday life: Self-defense training can teach you skills that you can use in your everyday life. For example, you can learn how to deal with bullies, how to protect yourself from muggers, and how to defend yourself in a carjacking.

Gymnastics

Gymnastics is an excellent foundation for martial arts training. It helps to develop strength, flexibility, coordination, and balance, which are all essential for martial arts. In addition, gymnastics can help to improve mental discipline and focus, which are also important for martial arts. Seminars on Gymnastic techniques will be organized to our students, by experienced and skilled guest instructors from across academies.

Here are some of the benefits of Gymnastics training from our institute:

Improved strength and power: Gymnastics helps to build strength in all the major muscle groups. This strength is essential for martial arts, as it is needed to perform techniques such as punches, kicks, and throws. Improved flexibility: Gymnastics helps to improve flexibility in all the major joints. This flexibility is essential for martial arts, as it allows martial artists to move quickly and easily. Improved coordination: Gymnastics helps to improve coordination and balance. This coordination is essential for martial arts, as it allows martial artists to execute techniques with precision and accuracy. Improved mental discipline and focus: Gymnastics helps to improve mental discipline and focus. This mental discipline is essential for martial arts, as it allows martial artists to stay focused and calm during competition.

Nanchaku

The nunchaku is most widely used in martial arts as a training weapon, since it allows the development of quicker mind / body coordination and teaches 3 dimensional movements and patterns.

What you will learn:

  • The beginners or foundational Nunchaku form performed with a single weapon
  • How to bounce the Nunchaku off your body for fast changes of direction.
  • Switching the weapon form one hand to the other with no pause in movements.
  • Spinning and figure eight spinning in vertical and horizontal planes.
  • The Intermediate double Nunchaku form with many symmetrical double strikes.
  • Advanced double Nunchaku with asymmetrical spins and figure eight spins with fast body spins and steps.
  • Complex asymmetrical strikes and figure eight spins while turning and transferring the strike directions.
  • Hand rolling Nunchaku with both hands while constantly changing spin direction.
